Project: Satellite-Based Drilling Activity as an Alternative Data Signal for Energy Equity Trading

What We Are Building
A multi-agent system that uses free Sentinel-1 SAR satellite imagery (with Sentinel-2 optical as a verification layer) to detect oil drilling activity in the Permian Basin (Delaware Basin in West Texas and southeast New Mexico, plus Midland Basin in West Texas, defined per EIA Permian Basin boundary) and converts that detection into a long-only trading signal for publicly traded US oil producers. The system predicts whether a company's upcoming quarterly **revenue** will beat or miss Wall Street analyst consensus, then trades on that prediction before earnings are reported. The target variable is locked to revenue. The chosen target reflects two binding constraints: (i) the project requirement explicitly mandates testing whether the alternative-data factor improves performance beyond momentum, value, quality, carry, and sentiment baselines, which requires a tradable financial signal (not signal-validation only); (ii) the user's WRDS subscription does not include `tr_ibeskpi` (the I/B/E/S KPI database with production-volume forecasts), but does include `tr_ibes` (standard EPS / revenue / sales detail-history forecasts). The drilling-to-revenue chain is modeled explicitly with a documented lag (drilling activity in quarter Q-1 informs revenue in quarter Q+1, via production with a spud-to-first-oil delay times realized oil price). Known revenue contaminants (WTI price level, hedge positions, NGL mix, M&A) are addressed by required residualization of returns against WTI and XLE, by inclusion of WTI-based and financial-factor baselines, and by acknowledged scope limitations in the paper rather than ignored.

Why This Matters
Companies like Orbital Insight and Kayrros sell satellite-based oil and gas intelligence to hedge funds for six figures annually. We are testing whether a free, reproducible version using Sentinel-1 SAR and Sentinel-2 imagery can produce a directionally useful trading signal that improves measured portfolio performance beyond standard financial-factor baselines (momentum, value, quality, carry-equivalents, sentiment) — the explicit test required by the project specification. We do not need to be perfectly accurate. If our satellite-based revenue estimate predicts the direction of revenue surprises (beat or miss) even 55-60% of the time, AND the resulting trades produce risk-adjusted returns that are not fully explained by exposure to those baseline factors plus WTI, that demonstrates the alternative data has incremental investable value.

Investment Universe
10 Permian-focused publicly traded oil producers: Diamondback Energy (FANG), EOG Resources (EOG), Devon Energy (DVN), Coterra Energy (CTRA), Occidental Petroleum (OXY), Matador Resources (MTDR), Permian Resources (PR), Ovintiv (OVV), SM Energy (SM), Crescent Energy (CRGY). We focus on Permian-concentrated companies because their production is most directly tied to Texas drilling activity, making the satellite signal most predictive. We exclude global majors like ExxonMobil and Chevron because Texas drilling is a small fraction of their total output. The system is long-only to sidestep small-cap borrow availability and short cost issues, and to keep backtest execution realistic.

The Signal We Are Creating
Our satellite-derived signal follows this chain. Sentinel-1 SAR imagery is captured every 12 days (single-satellite revisit after Sentinel-1B failed in December 2021; restored cadence after Sentinel-1C launched in December 2024) over known Permian Basin drilling areas. Because Sentinel-1 is radar-based, it penetrates cloud cover and detects bare-earth disturbance directly, which is well-suited to the semi-arid Permian where vegetation indices alone would be noisy. Sentinel-2 optical imagery (NDVI and normalized difference indices) is used as a verification layer when sky is clear. The monitored site universe is constructed point-in-time: at each decision date T, only pads whose permits were filed before T are eligible for monitoring; the universe expands quarterly as new permits enter the public record. This prevents look-ahead bias from including future-discovered pad coordinates in past-date detection runs. We classify each monitored site as newly active (fresh disturbance detected), continuously active (ongoing disturbance), or idle (stable surface, no activity). We aggregate detections per company per quarter by mapping site coordinates to operator ownership using Texas Railroad Commission historical operator records at permit filing date (used only for coordinate mapping, not as a trading signal; mapping is point-in-time to avoid drift from acreage swaps). We translate active site counts and quarter-over-quarter changes into a quarterly revenue forecast per company through an explicit two-step chain: (1) drilling activity in quarter Q-1 produces incremental production in quarters Q and Q+1 via a spud-to-first-oil lag and an EIA Permian type-curve decline assumption applied to existing wells; (2) the resulting production volume forecast is multiplied by the realized average WTI price for the target quarter (using only WTI prints publicly available before T-14) plus a company-specific historical realized-price differential to produce a point-estimate quarterly revenue forecast. We compare our revenue estimate to point-in-time Wall Street consensus revenue reconstructed from the I/B/E/S Detail History file (`tr_ibes`, sales/revenue measure) via WRDS as of T-14 days before each earnings date. If our revenue estimate is more than 10% above consensus, the signal is "beat" (go long). If more than 10% below, the signal is "miss" (no trade, since we are long-only). If within 10%, no trade. The 10% threshold is the headline default; threshold sweep (5%, 10%, 15%, 20%) is in the Robustness Checks section. The trading layer is gated by site-level SAR validation: before any backtest, the GIS Detection Agent's site-level outputs are validated against TRC spud and completion records on a sample of 50-100 sites to confirm that detected change events correspond to drilling events (rather than road grading, equipment movement, rainfall changes, or workovers). Per DL #48 (project-goal alignment) and the revised Pre-Code Action Item 2, weak precision/recall does NOT trigger a hard pivot; it is reported transparently in the Limitations section and the trading layer continues. This supersedes the earlier hard-gate language in DL #29 and DL #37.

Multi-Agent Architecture (5 Agents)
Agent 1: GIS Detection Agent. Processes Sentinel-1 SAR imagery (and Sentinel-2 optical for verification) via Google Earth Engine Python API. Runs change detection on monitored coordinates. Outputs a list of sites classified as newly active, continuously active, or idle, with timestamps. LLM: none needed, this is a computational pipeline.

Agent 2: Revenue Forecast Agent. Receives site classifications from Agent 1. Aggregates active site counts per company per quarter, applies the deterministic drilling-to-production-to-revenue chain (production lag with EIA Permian type-curve decline; production × pre-T-14 average WTI × company-specific historical realized-price differential), and produces a quarterly revenue forecast per company. LLM: Gemini 2.5 Flash interprets activity trends and produces a written revenue outlook; the LLM does not generate the numerical forecast — the number is deterministic and governed by the locked forecast-model spec.

Agent 3: Consensus Comparison Agent. Pulls point-in-time analyst consensus revenue from the I/B/E/S Detail History file (`tr_ibes`, sales/revenue measure) via WRDS for each company as of T-14 days before earnings. Compares our satellite-derived revenue estimate to consensus revenue. Flags companies where the divergence exceeds 10% above consensus (long candidates). LLM: Llama 70B via Groq (evaluates divergence and assigns confidence level: high, medium, low).

Agent 4: News Verification Agent. For each flagged company, reads recent news from GDELT to check whether the satellite-detected drilling pattern has been disclosed in GDELT-indexed public news prior to T-14. Hard-coded to use only GDELT data up to T-14 days (the position open date); no data after that point enters the agent. If GDELT contains an article describing a consistent drilling-activity change before T-14, the signal is downgraded by one conviction tier. The agent's scope is limited to GDELT-indexed news; sell-side research, investor decks, transcripts, and state filings are out of scope and listed as a Limitations item in the paper. LLM: Llama 70B via Groq (classifies GDELT articles for consistency with the satellite-detected pattern, not "full market knowledge").

Agent 5: Investment Board (with absorbed Risk Manager functions). Three members using different LLMs: Bull Advocate (Gemini) argues for the strongest trade cases, Bear Advocate (Llama) argues against and identifies risks, Risk Arbiter (DeepSeek R1 via Cerebras) mediates and makes the final decision. The board chooses which trades to take, not how to size them. Position sizing follows locked, explicit risk rules: maximum position size 15% per name, maximum 8 names held simultaneously out of the 10-name universe, conviction-to-size mapping (high conviction 15%, medium 10%, low 5%, no trade 0%), and cash position when no signals parked in BIL (SPDR Bloomberg 1-3 Month T-Bill ETF) as the short-term Treasury proxy. When more than 8 names trigger in the same earnings season, selection priority is conviction tier first (high before medium before low) and divergence magnitude (larger satellite-vs-consensus gap) as the tiebreaker within a tier. Each member sees the satellite detections, production estimates, consensus comparisons, and news verification results.

What We Are Measuring
Primary metric: revenue-surprise prediction accuracy. For each company each quarter, did our satellite-derived revenue estimate correctly predict whether actual reported revenue came in above analyst consensus? We report hit rate (percentage of correct directional predictions among triggered trades), average return per trade (mean P&L of positions taken based on our signal), cumulative portfolio return over the backtest period, annualized Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown. Stock returns are computed as total return with dividends reinvested (relevant for OXY, DVN, FANG, EOG and other dividend-paying names in the universe). Given an expected trade count of approximately 30-40 long trades over 20 quarters (long-only system, only "beat" signals trigger entries), all results are reported with bootstrap confidence intervals rather than point estimates. Because trades within a Permian-only universe are not independent (the 10 names co-move on oil beta and often report in the same earnings season), inference uses clustered bootstrap rather than naive trade-level resampling: primary specification is firm-clustered bootstrap (resample by company, keeping all of that company's trades together), with quarter-block bootstrap (resample by earnings season) reported as a sensitivity. A pre-registered primary test is declared before the run; threshold sweeps, baseline comparisons, and ablation results are reported as secondary findings with appropriate multiple-comparison context.

What We Are Comparing Against (10 Long-Only Strategies)
The baseline set is expanded from 7 to 10 to satisfy the project requirement that the alternative-data factor be tested against momentum, value, quality, carry-equivalents, and sentiment baselines. All baselines are long-only, applied to the same point-in-time tradable universe, with the same risk rules (15% max per name, 8 names max simultaneously) and 30 bps round-trip transaction cost. New financial-factor baselines (Strategies 8-10) replace the prior signal-only "carry/yield" placeholder in the energy context with their closest implementable analogues.

Strategy 1: Full satellite pipeline with Investment Board (long-only). Our main system. All 5 agents active, satellite-derived revenue signal as the primary input, board debates and finalizes trades.
Strategy 2: Full satellite pipeline without the News Verification Agent (long-only). Tests whether checking GDELT-visible public news for the satellite-detected pattern matters. If performance is similar without Agent 4, news verification is unnecessary.
Strategy 3: Analyst-revision follower (long-only sentiment-of-experts baseline). Go long companies where analyst revenue consensus from `tr_ibes` was revised upward in the 4 weeks before T-14. No satellite data, no agents. Tests whether following analyst momentum is sufficient.
Strategy 4: Oil price momentum (long-only momentum baseline, macro). Go long all eligible names when WTI crude has positive 3-month momentum at T-14, go to cash when momentum is negative.
Strategy 5: Baker Hughes basin rig count (long-only alt-data-without-satellite baseline). Use the free weekly Baker Hughes Permian-basin rig count, applied uniformly to the universe: go long the equal-weighted universe when the basin rig count is rising on a 4-week-on-4-week basis at T-14, go to cash otherwise. This is the most important alt-data comparison because Permian basin rig count is the closest existing free public proxy for what our satellite imagery detects. If our satellite signal cannot beat the basin-aggregate rig count, the satellite data adds no incremental value over what is freely public every Friday. Treated as a legitimate publishable null result if it occurs. (DL #45 documents that operator-level rig count data is paid and out of scope.)
Strategy 6: Equal-weighted universe (long-only). Hold all eligible names in equal weight every quarter. Tests whether name selection adds value at all over naive diversification within the universe.
Strategy 7: XLE buy and hold. Buy the energy sector ETF and hold for the entire backtest period. The passive sector benchmark. Acknowledged as a tough benchmark; the value proposition is framed as "match XLE with fewer holdings and a better Sharpe ratio" rather than "beat XLE outright on absolute return."
Strategy 8: Cross-sectional 12-1 stock momentum (long-only momentum baseline, single-stock). At each T-14, rank the eligible universe by trailing-12-month total return excluding the most recent month, hold the top 4 names equal-weighted, rebalance quarterly. The standard single-stock momentum factor.
Strategy 9: Value composite (long-only value baseline). At each T-14, rank the eligible universe by an equal-weighted composite of EV/EBITDA, P/B, and FCF yield (Compustat fundamentals via WRDS, lagged to most recent reported quarter to ensure point-in-time discipline), hold the top 4 names equal-weighted, rebalance quarterly. The standard value factor adapted to single-stock energy.
Strategy 10: Quality composite (long-only quality baseline). At each T-14, rank the eligible universe by an equal-weighted composite of trailing-12-month return-on-equity, debt-to-equity (inverted, lower is better), and operating-cash-flow margin (Compustat fundamentals via WRDS, lagged), hold the top 4 names equal-weighted, rebalance quarterly. The standard quality factor adapted to single-stock energy.

Backtest Design
Time period: Q1 2021 to Q4 2025 (20 quarterly earnings cycles). The window starts post-COVID to skip the March-April 2020 negative-oil-price shock and Permian shut-in, which would otherwise dominate signal-to-noise. Lost regime analysis from skipping 2020 is compensated by splitting the 2021-2025 sample by oil price regime (high vs low oil price periods) and drilling cycle (expansion vs contraction phases). Starting capital: $1,000,000. Transaction costs: 30 basis points round-trip. Trading schedule: positions are opened 2 weeks before each company's earnings date and closed 2 days after earnings are reported, giving a holding period of approximately 2-3 weeks per trade. Backtest methodology: single-pass backtest over the full 20-quarter window with regime-split robustness checks (oil price high vs low, drilling expansion vs contraction). Walk-forward / rolling-window evaluation is not used; the limited 20-quarter sample makes a single-pass backtest with explicit regime splits and bootstrap confidence intervals more interpretable than walk-forward folds with thin out-of-sample windows. LLM agent prompts and pipeline parameters are frozen before the backtest run to prevent in-sample tuning. LLM determinism and reproducibility controls: temperature is set to 0 for all agents; model versions are pinned to specific releases (e.g., gemini-2.5-flash-002, llama-3.1-70b-versatile, deepseek-r1-distill-llama-70b) rather than floating aliases; all agent outputs use structured-output validation (JSON schemas) so malformed responses are caught rather than silently parsed; a pre-backtest stability check runs each agent's prompt 5 times on a fixed input set and confirms outputs are identical or near-identical, with any variance documented. These specifications, prompt texts, model versions, API parameters, and data hashes are recorded in a reproducibility manifest checked into the repository. Pipeline timing sanity check: drilling activity in quarter Q produces production in Q and Q+1, earnings for quarter Q are reported approximately 45 days after Q-close, and trades open at T-14 (~Q-close + 30 days), so satellite data used at trade time is 30-120 days old, which is appropriate because we are predicting a quarter whose drilling activity has already occurred.

Data Sources (All Free or WRDS-Available)
Satellite imagery: Sentinel-1 SAR primary and Sentinel-2 optical verification, both via Google Earth Engine (free, 10-meter resolution, 12-day SAR revisit and 5-day optical revisit). Site universe is built point-in-time from Texas Railroad Commission and New Mexico OCD permit data: at each historical decision date T, only pads whose permits were filed before T are included in monitoring. The full point-in-time universe over 2021-2025 covers approximately 500-1,000 active drill pads across the highest-activity Permian counties (Midland, Martin, Reeves, Loving, Howard, Reagan in Texas; Eddy and Lea in New Mexico), but the eligible subset at any given backtest decision date is strictly smaller, expanding quarterly as new permits enter the public record. GEE free tier (25,000 EECU-hours per month, non-commercial) is sufficient for 500-1,000 sites at 12-day SAR revisit (roughly 2-3 captures per month per site) plus marginal Sentinel-2 verification cost; if quota is hit, scope down to top 300 sites by historical drilling intensity. Oil prices: EIA (free, weekly WTI spot and futures prices). Analyst consensus estimates: I/B/E/S Detail History (`tr_ibes`) via WRDS, sales/revenue measure code, point-in-time reconstruction as of T-14 (no leakage). The user's WRDS subscription does not include `tr_ibeskpi` (production-volume KPI database), which is why revenue rather than production volume is the target variable. Drilling site coordinates and operator history: Texas Railroad Commission and New Mexico OCD (free, historical operator records at permit filing date). News: GDELT (free, article-level timestamps, event-type tagging; T-14 hard cutoff enforced). Rig counts: Baker Hughes (free, weekly, used only for the comparison strategy, not in our main system). Stock price data: Yahoo Finance (free, total return with dividends reinvested, for computing returns and benchmarks). Earnings calendar (announcement dates for T-14 anchor): Yahoo Finance earnings calendar with cross-check against SEC EDGAR 8-K filing dates for the actual report timestamp. Revenue actuals (label for hit-rate evaluation): SEC EDGAR 10-Q filings and 8-K earnings press releases (point-in-time accurate via filing date), with company-reported revenue from the income statement; cross-checked against Compustat fundamentals via WRDS. Production-volume actuals are still extracted from the same EDGAR sources for use in the satellite forecast chain (the intermediate physical signal) and for residualization diagnostics, but they are no longer the prediction target. Earnings calendar source discipline: announcement dates are sourced from company investor-relations earnings calendars, which are typically pre-announced 30+ days ahead; T-14 entry uses the date as known at T-14, not the ex-post finalized announcement timestamp. The Yahoo Finance / SEC EDGAR cross-check is used only to verify accuracy of the date after the fact, not to anchor the trade. Financial-factor baseline data: stock returns and momentum from CRSP via WRDS; valuation and quality fundamentals (EV/EBITDA, P/B, FCF yield, ROE, debt-to-equity, operating-cash-flow margin) from Compustat via WRDS, lagged to most recent reported quarter for point-in-time integrity.

Research Gap We Are Addressing
We address the multimodal integration gap: finance research has many papers on alternative data and many papers on multi-agent LLM systems, but very little work combining satellite/geospatial data with LLM-based interpretation in a rigorous and testable investment framework. We also address the attribution gap by testing whether the multi-agent board improves decisions versus a single agent, and whether the satellite signal adds value beyond freely available traditional signals (analyst consensus, oil price momentum, Baker Hughes rig counts).

Ablation Tests
Full pipeline with board versus single generalist agent receiving all the same data (tests multi-agent coordination value). Full pipeline with satellite data versus full pipeline without satellite data relying only on news and consensus (tests whether satellite data adds value). Board (3 sub-agents debating) versus solo portfolio manager (1 agent deciding) (tests debate mechanism value). Diverse-model board (Gemini, Llama, DeepSeek) versus same-model board (all Gemini) (tests model diversity value, optional if time permits).

Robustness Checks
Signal threshold sweep: re-run the main strategy at 5%, 10% (default), 15%, and 20% satellite-vs-consensus divergence thresholds to confirm the 10% default is not a cherry-picked sweet spot and to characterize the trade-count vs hit-rate tradeoff. Regime splits: report metrics separately for high vs low oil price regimes and for drilling expansion vs contraction phases within the 2021-2025 window. Site-universe sensitivity: spot-check that the top-300 fallback (used if GEE quota is exceeded) produces directionally similar signals to the full 500-1,000 site set on a sample of quarters.

Placebo and residualization tests: (a) Non-Permian placebo — run the full pipeline on a control universe of US E&P companies whose drilling is concentrated outside the Permian (Marcellus / Appalachia and Bakken-focused names such as RRC, AR, EQT, CHRD, CIVI). The Permian satellite signal should have no predictive power for non-Permian operators; if it does, the apparent alpha is spurious. (b) Random-coordinate placebo — replace the permit-derived monitored sites with random Permian-area coordinates and confirm signal performance collapses. (c) Future-data placebo — feed satellite data dated after the trade decision into the pipeline; results should not improve, otherwise leakage is suspected. (d) Return residualization — regress portfolio returns on XLE total return and WTI returns at trade horizon, and report alpha and t-statistic on the residual; the headline performance is reported both gross and as residual alpha so sector-beta exposure is separated from operational-information value.

Temporal Discipline
Sentinel-1 SAR and Sentinel-2 imagery use the Google Earth Engine asset publication (ingestion) date as the public-availability timestamp, not the satellite capture date. Capture and ingestion can differ by hours to several days due to processing, orbit corrections, and product reprocessing; the backtest treats imagery as available only from its GEE asset-publication timestamp onward. Oil prices use the EIA publication date. Analyst estimates use the I/B/E/S effective date as of T-14 before earnings. 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K data use the SEC filing date. News uses the GDELT article publication timestamp with a hard T-14 cutoff. Earnings announcement dates use the date as pre-announced in company IR earnings calendars before T-14 (most are scheduled 30+ days ahead); ex-post finalized timestamps are not used to anchor entries. Texas Railroad Commission and New Mexico OCD operator mapping uses the historical operator-of-record at permit filing date, not current operator. The monitored site universe is itself point-in-time: only permits filed before decision date T are eligible. No data point is used before its public availability date. A complete timestamp log is maintained and included as an appendix in the final paper.

Design Decisions Log
This log captures the design concerns raised during project scoping and the resolution applied to each, so that every choice in the system is traceable to a documented motivation.

Round 1 concerns (initial scoping):
1. Sample size. Original 10-quarter window produced approximately 30-50 trades after threshold filtering, too thin for statistical power. Resolution: extended backtest window and adopted bootstrap confidence interval reporting.
2. Drilling-to-revenue lag. Original "active sites times WTI" formula ignored the 3-6 month spud-to-first-oil delay and the months-long completion lag. Resolution: pivoted target variable from revenue to production volume, which uses the natural drilling-to-production lag chain (drilling in Q produces volume in Q and Q+1) without requiring oil price modeling.
3. Point-in-time consensus. Yahoo Finance only exposes current consensus, not historical timestamped snapshots, creating a leakage risk. Resolution: I/B/E/S detail history file via WRDS, reconstructed point-in-time as of T-14 before each earnings date.
4. Sentinel-2 weakness in Permian. Semi-arid sparse vegetation produces weak NDVI change signal, and West Texas cloud cover further degrades optical detection. Resolution: switched primary imagery to Sentinel-1 SAR (cloud-penetrating, detects bare-earth disturbance directly), retained Sentinel-2 as verification layer when sky is clear.
5. Operator-to-pad mapping drift. Permian acreage swaps and sales mean current operator-of-record differs from operator at drill date. Resolution: Texas Railroad Commission historical operator records at permit filing date, point-in-time mapping documented.
6. Agent count overhead. Original 7-agent design created attribution overhead and contained overlapping responsibilities. Resolution: merged Production Estimation and Revenue Estimation into a single Production Forecast Agent, absorbed Risk Manager functions into the Investment Board, final count 5 agents.
7. News Verification timing. Risk that GDELT data after the trade-open date enters the agent and creates leakage. Resolution: T-14 hard cutoff enforced in code, no GDELT article with publication timestamp after T-14 is read by the agent.
8. Baker Hughes baseline risk. If satellite signal cannot beat free weekly rig counts, the project's headline value proposition collapses. Resolution: accepted that this would still be a publishable null result with attribution value, explicitly framed as legitimate research output.
9. Universe size and shorting. Small-cap names in the universe have limited borrow availability and high short cost. Resolution: long-only system, removes execution friction and makes backtest realistic.

Round 2 concerns (post-refinement):
10. COVID regime contamination. Including 2020 would let the negative oil price shock and Permian shut-in dominate signal-to-noise. Resolution: window starts Q1 2021 (post-COVID); lost regime variation compensated by within-window splits on oil price regime and drilling cycle phase.
11. I/B/E/S production coverage uncertainty. Production volume consensus may not have at least 3-5 analysts per quarter for small-cap names. Resolution: open item, coverage to be verified on WRDS before locking target variable; explicit fallback to revenue prediction with drilling-to-revenue lag model documented.
12. Sentinel-1 revisit cadence gap. Sentinel-1B failure in December 2021 reduced revisit from 6 to 12 days until Sentinel-1C launched in December 2024. Resolution: documented; 12-day cadence judged adequate for quarterly trading decisions.
13. Long-only baseline math. With short trades removed, "miss" signals become no-trade rather than short, and benchmark math changes. Resolution: all 7 comparison strategies updated to long-only versions; value proposition reframed as "match XLE with fewer holdings and better Sharpe ratio" rather than "beat XLE on absolute return."
14. Trade count tightness. Approximately 30-40 long trades over 20 quarters (revised down from the long-short estimate of roughly 70 once the system was made long-only and only "beat" signals trigger entries) is workable but not large. Resolution: bootstrap confidence intervals reported on all metrics, no point-estimate-only reporting.
15. Pipeline timing sanity check. Need to confirm satellite data used at trade time is appropriately old, not future-leaking. Resolution: full timing chain documented (drilling Q produces volume Q and Q+1, earnings reported Q-close + 45 days, trade opens at T-14 which is approximately Q-close + 30 days, so satellite data is 30-120 days old at trade time).
16. Investment Board discretion drift. Allowing the board to set position sizes risks unattributable performance. Resolution: locked explicit risk rules (15% max per name, 8 names max simultaneously, conviction-to-size mapping high/medium/low/none = 15%/10%/5%/0%, cash parked in short-term Treasury proxy when no signals); board chooses which trades, not how to size them.

Round 4 concerns (Codex independent review):
27. Trade independence and inference. The 30-40 expected long trades are not independent: the 10 Permian names co-move on oil beta and often report in overlapping earnings seasons, and the robustness menu (7 strategies × 4 thresholds × multiple ablations × multiple metrics) introduces multiple-testing degrees of freedom. Resolution: replaced naive trade-level bootstrap with firm-clustered bootstrap (resample by company, keeping all of that company's trades together) as the primary inference, with quarter-block bootstrap as a sensitivity; declared a pre-registered primary test before the run; threshold sweeps, baselines, and ablations reported as secondary findings with multiple-comparison context.
28. Point-in-time site-universe leakage. Building the monitored site list from the full 2019-2025 permit set and applying it to 2021 imagery introduces look-ahead bias because the search space is filtered ex post to economically relevant coordinates.
